About

I’m a product designer with a background in research and writing. After studying comparative literature at Washington University in St. Louis, I moved to Italy to pursue my interests in photography and design. Since then, I’ve worked as a designer for startups in New York and DC.

I’m most interested in understanding the political and economic context of design; after five years of working as a designer, I’ve learned that our economic system guides what we design and build. So I’m currently studying for a master’s in economics at The New School of Social Research, where I want to question the assumptions embedded in design:

  • How might we create and judge value in new ways, especially that which we cannot easily measure or monetize?
  • How might we design a socially and ecologically sustainable economic system?
  • How might we define development and progress in new ways?
