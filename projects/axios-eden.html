Eden CMS

After redesigning Axios.com for search engine optimization, we improved our content management system to accommodate the new site.

Year: 2020-2021
My role: Product designer
Credits: Al Lucca (VP of design), Kasiana McLenaghan (senior product manager)
Live: axios.com
The updated Eden CMS.

The Problem

Editors and writers at Axios actually enjoy using our content management system (Eden) – a rarity in media organizations. I led a redesign of Axios.com that launched in January 2020, and so we needed to update Eden to accommodate the new site. We took this opportunity to also reflect our new brand guidelines and address users’ pain points in Eden, while maintaining what already makes Eden delightful to use: its simplicity and elegance.

Writing & Editing

After interviewing writers and editors, I restructured Eden’s story editor. Improvements included: 1) Reorganizing all metadata settings into distinct groupings in a side navigation (in order to create hierarchy so that users, who enter the story editor for different tasks, can find what they’re looking for more quickly); 2) Adding a visual indicator to required fields (in order to clarify requirements for publishing); 3) Relocating the main actions to persist in the upper right corner across all pages of the editor (in order to allow users to publish at any stage). I then incorporated new functionality and removed unnecessary functionality for the new Axios.com into this structure.

Curation

Previously, Eden acted as a writing and editing tool only. I created a new pattern for curation (a focus of the Axios.com redesign) in Eden. Adding a side navigation to Eden’s dashboard allowed for new workflows in Eden, including curation. I started by designing curation for the Axios.com homepage, but we ended up launching curatorial tools for Deep Dives first. The system of curation in Eden is flexible enough for any type of editorial curation: of our homepage, section pages, subsection pages, “Deep Dives,” etc. Allowing editors to curate these pages and projects in Eden saves developers from having to do the work manually.
