Panoply Tech Blog

Panoply's tech blog is a place for our design and engineering team to share inspiration and process.

Background

Our tech team was looking for a way to build our team brand, both internally within our company and externally in the industry. So another designer and I decided to collaborate with engineering to create a team blog. Our intention is to use the platform to push ourselves to build new skills, to reflect on our process and values, and to connect with the larger design and tech community.

Research

We began the project with a competitive analysis, looking at blogs from design and product teams at InVision, MailChimp, Slack, Intercom, Dropbox, AirBnb and Asana. We noted what we liked and didn't like about both their content and design. While about half of the companies we looked at hosted their blog on Medium, we decided to design and code our own – both to exercise these skills, and to create a customized experience.

Wireframes

Because our goal for the blog was to differentiate our brand from other similar sites, we spent a lot of time in the ideation phase trying to push past surface level ideas to something more original. Using the 6-8-5 ideation exercise (6 to 8 ideas in 5 minutes), we sketched out different possibilities for the site's various pages.

6-8-5 exercise

Style Direction

We wanted the site's aesthetic to reflect our own taste, since the project started as an opportunity to challenge ourselves as designers. We decided that aesthetic meant: sophisticated, young, thoughtful, curated and minimal. We drew inspiration from other blog sites, travel photography and print magazines.
