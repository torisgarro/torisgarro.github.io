AsylumConnect

The AsylumConnect catalog is a national database of resources for LGTBQ asylum seekers and the organizations who serve them.

Background

I led the redesign of the organization’s catalog site. Our main goals for the redesign were to expand the catalog's scope from two cities (Seattle and Philadelphia) to six cities (adding NYC, DC, San Francisco and LA), and to make the site mobile responsive. We also wanted to provide improved functionality, defined in our research process.

Research

The catalog site presented a unique challenge from a UX perspective: users come to the site with vastly different goals, access to technology and English language proficiency. To address this challenge, we began conducting surveys and remote user interviews. We analyzed the results to identify four user personas: an asylum seeker, a service provider, a volunteer and a donor. We also identified the specific features and screens we would need to design.

Sketches

Wireframes

Based on our research, we created wireframes. We then tested these with users remotely, synthesizing feedback and continuing to iterate on our designs.

Mockups

In the end, our MVP addressed the needs found in our research. New and improved functionality includes: a Google translate feature, additional search filters, the ability to create a user account, the ability to save a resource to a favorites list, and improved information about resources.

The AsylumConnect catalog has been recognized by the United Nations, the Clinton Global Initiative University, Forbes and Harvard University.
