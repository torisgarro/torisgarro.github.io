Megaphone

Megaphone is a dynamic ad platform for podcast publishers, including Gimlet, Slate and HowStuffWorks. I led the design of features to forecast ad impressions in Megaphone.

The Problem

Before this project, Megaphone allowed clients to sell ad inventory by offering ad placement in a podcast over time (e.g. “Casper’s ad will run in these specific four episodes of Revisionist History for three weeks”). But we wanted to sign a top network who sells ad inventory in a different way: by selling ad impressions over time (e.g. “Casper’s ad will run in any episode of Revisionist History until 100,000 people listen to it”).

Strategy

In order to introduce this new mode of selling into Megaphone, we needed to forecast the number of future impressions available on a podcast show or episode. We also needed to enable sellers to book these impressions and pace their delivery over time.

User Interviews

For context, we interviewed a handful of our enterprise clients about their existing ad sales processes, thinking about how this new way of selling ad inventory could fit into their workflow. We learned that they used internal spreadsheets to track their ad inventory, and they used their own data to forecast future available impressions. These conversations confirmed that we were addressing the right design problem with this feature set. We also learned that some clients use multiple modes of selling ad inventory in tandem.

Searching

Our initial research revealed that searching for and viewing relevant ad inventory would be crucial to selling by impressions. We explored different search filters and ways of visualizing search results. Based on our interviews, we knew that users were interested not just in looking up future unsold impressions, but also future sold impressions. This is because users still view sold inventory as “available” ad space, since they often swap out ads if needed. Our final data visualization of forecasted impressions reflects this insight.

Wireframes for the search page

Don't Overcomplicate

After testing wireframes with users, we realized our design’s many filter options actually narrowed their inventory search results too quickly. At this point in their process, they were still exploring their options – they didn’t want to be limited by anything as specific as the position or geo-target of their ad.

When we removed the unnecessary filters, we found that our search page was beginning to look a lot like another existing screen in our platform – our reports. We ended up adapting our report design to this use case, instead of creating an entirely new flow, as we’d originally planned. Though this solution was a bit of a compromise in design, it allowed us to use existing components in the platform, making more efficient use of our developer’s time and allowing us to meet our release deadline.

Launch

With the successful launch of this feature set, we signed a top network to Megaphone – adding 50 million monthly downloads, thereby doubling our ad inventory. We now also support a new mode of selling ad inventory that is widely believed to be the future of the podcast industry – establishing us as an industry leader and opening the door to future clients who sell in this way.
