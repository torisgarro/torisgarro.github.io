We created the Emerging Voices interview series to explore the day-to-day experiences and perspectives of emerging designers.

The Problem

AIGA, the national design association, has historically struggled to reach designers at the beginning of their careers – just when they might most benefit from AIGA's programming and network. So AIGA’s Diversity & Inclusion task force launched Emerge 2.0, an initiative that offers a series of events, experiences and content tailored to emerging designers (designers within the first five years of their career).

Emerging DC

In order to generate awareness of the Emerge 2.0 launch, we created Emerging Voices, a multimedia series spotlighting emerging designers in DC. The DC series served as a national pilot for chapters across the country: other chapters can execute similar content, engaging creatives in their own communities.

We interviewed 18 emerging designers in the DC area over the course of three months. We aimed to showcase the diversity of local talent, featuring creatives of differing nationalities, ethnicities, genders, orientations, ages, physical abilities, educational backgrounds and roles in the design industry.

Our Reach

AIGA featured the interview series in various formats across their social channels (YouTube, Instagram, Facebook, LinkedIn, Snapchat), and on the AIGA Emerge Blog.

The social media campaign reached a total of 1M+ people. Over 87,000 audience members – more than four times the size of the organization’s membership count – engaged to consume content, react or share with their peers.
